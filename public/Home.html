Blood Donation

According to the World Health Organisation (WHO), voluntary non-remunerated blood donation is the surest approach to ensure sufficient supply of safe blood to meet national requirement of blood transfusion.Blood donation is the most important social service to humankind. by donating we can give others a life support. Blood donation at the right time can save millions of lifes all over the world every year.


Blood Donation Advice:

  • Donation frequency should be Every 56 days from Donation.
  • You must be in good health condition.
  • You must weigh at least 50 KG
  • You must be at least 16 years above to donate

Tips for the day of your donation:

  • Drink an extra 1 litre of water (or other nonalcoholic drink) before donating blood.
  • Eat a healthy meal, avoiding fatty foods like fries or ice cream.
  • Don’t do any heavy lifting or vigorous exercise for the rest of the day
  • Keep eating iron-rich foods.
  • If you donate frequently, be sure to take multivitamins with iron to ensure you continue to replenish your iron stores before your next donation.
